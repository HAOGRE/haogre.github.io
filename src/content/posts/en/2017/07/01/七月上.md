<center>

I turn to dust and soar, chasing the naked flight against the wind,
rushing toward July's scaffold, time scorching and searing.

Memory shreds every fantasy, the road fills with hurrying feet,
rare and precious in this world, the field of fragrant magnetism spreads.

I wish to ride the wind and break through the waves, to tread the golden sands and seas,
rather a misunderstanding than to let my courage go to waste.

I wish you were a lie, that you'd never appeared at the southern wall,
a smile is god's disguise, a smile is pain held back.

Let me walk toward you, walk toward your bed,
let me see you, see your wounds.

I imagine you standing there, standing at the edge of the desert frontier,
I imagine you standing there, standing in July.

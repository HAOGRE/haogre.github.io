<h2 id="important-notice">An Important Notice to iCloud Users in Mainland China</h2>
<p>Dear customer,<br>Thank you for using iCloud. We are writing to share some important information about iCloud services in mainland China.<br>Apple has announced a partnership with Guizhou-Cloud Big Data Industry Co., Ltd. (GCBD) to operate iCloud services for customers in mainland China. This will help us continue to improve the speed and reliability of iCloud in China, and to comply with Chinese regulations.</p>

<p>Starting February 28, 2018, iCloud services associated with your Apple ID will be operated by GCBD. All data you store through iCloud — including photos, videos, documents, and backups — will be subject to the terms and conditions of iCloud (operated by GCBD). Other than this change, your iCloud service will continue without interruption.<br>If you are not a Chinese citizen residing in mainland China, you may update the country or region in your Apple ID settings to reflect your current location and continue using iCloud under Apple's existing terms and conditions. For more information, see the iCloud (operated by GCBD) FAQ.<br>If you are a Chinese citizen residing in mainland China but do not wish to use iCloud (operated by GCBD), you may disable your iCloud account at <a href="https://www.icloud.com/optout" target="_blank" rel="noopener">https://www.icloud.com/optout</a>.<br>If you have questions, please see the iCloud (operated by GCBD) <a href="https://support.apple.com/zh-cn/HT208352" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FAQ</a>. If you need help, contact Apple Support.<br>Sincerely,<br>Apple Support</p>

<h2 id="leaving-apple">Leaving the Apple Ecosystem</h2>
<p>Aside from my MacBook Pro, I'm no longer using any Apple devices (my wife still has an iPad). Apple's products are genuinely good — I've been a long-time user of sorts, going back through the shuffle, iPod, iPhone era.<br>But the closed ecosystem is what finally pushed me out. I couldn't take it anymore.<br>As an Android developer by hobby, MacBook Pro + Android actually works pretty well for me.</p>

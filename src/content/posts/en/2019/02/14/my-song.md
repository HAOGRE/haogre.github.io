<center>
What you want but cannot have —
what can you do against life?
What you should let go but can't —
you just keep rehashing the past.
By the time you realize time is a thief,
it has already stolen all your choices.

Love is nothing but a high fever,
longing is the cough that never quite heals.
It can't be forgiven
yet can't be stopped —
hatred climbs the wall in the night.
It's hollow and empty
yet humming and ringing —
who fired a cold shot into your heart?

An old love's vows are exactly like a slap —
every time you remember a line, you take a blow to the face.
Then for years you can't ask, can't smell a woman's perfume.

The past is not like smoke.
Yes —
nostalgia in love is no virtue either.
But love isn't like writing songs —
no matter how sincere, it never becomes a style.

Tell me, have you ever seen longing let anyone go?
Whether you're a repeat offender or have a clean record,
all I know is the ones who were together long enough parted —
I've never seen the long-parted reunite.

Time — don't rush me.
What should come, I won't push away.
What I owe, I'll pay. What I should give, I'll give.
Time — don't rush me.
What's gone far, I won't chase.
I only want to tell the whole story.

Who can tell me what this is?
His love buried and smoothed over in his heart for years — still carrying its force.
It can't be forgiven
yet can't be stopped —
love climbs the wall in the night.
It's hollow and empty
yet humming and ringing —
who fired a cold shot into your heart?

An old love's vows are exactly like a slap —
every time you remember a line, you take a blow to the face.
Then for years you can't ask, can't smell a woman's perfume.

What you want but cannot have —
what can you do against life?
What you want but cannot have —
there are no wise people in love.
